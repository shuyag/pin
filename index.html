MOTIVATION

How rapid is rapid prototyping?

The timescale for rapid prototyping has consistently been constrained by the manufacturing speeds of the machines used in additive or subtractive manufacturing. The aim in this project is to take a new approach to rapid prototyping by creating a digitally reconfigurable mold surface to be used in low pressure injection molding, or vacuum forming.

Traditional molding processes require there to either be a target shape already created through additive manufacturing methods such as 3D printing, or through subtractive methods, and then put into a container with pliable or pourable molding material, then left to cure. Alternatively, a mold is created with the same methods, using a CAM software. The time required for either method is significant and creates excess waste materials.

This project will involve significant study, experimentation, and implementation with mechanical actuation, force transfer, material properties, electronic controls and electromechanical systems, fluid properties, and heat transfer in order to create a higher resolution digitally reconfigurable surface that may be incorporated later into mold making techniques.

The motivation from this project draws largely from my experiences in product design and rapid prototyping, and the need for alternative and truly rapid prototyping techniques in a new, agile manufacturing age. While rapid prototyping solutions in the past have significantly cut down manufacturing time to a couple of days or several hours, a high resolution, digitally configurable surfaces would allow the rapid prototyping of looks-like feels-like prototypes of medium to high fidelity on a production timescale of a few hours.

This project focuses on creating a many voxel to one motor actuation technique combined with a flexible surface to improve the resolution possible on a mechanically actuated, electronically controlled surface that can withstand moderate heat and pressure. This resolution and price point should be comparable to that of a standard 3D desktop printer. The hope is that this will accelerate rapid prototyping to be truly rapid.
